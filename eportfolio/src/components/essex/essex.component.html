Why I chose Essex

During my time working in UK, I was desirous of improving my base education level and never got around to whole-heartedly looking into a relevant course. It was not until the coronavirus pandemic appeared, that I earnestly considered the meaning of life: what have I achieved for myself in the past years?

I spent many weeks looking at various online universities, such as:

  • Arden University
  • The Open University
  • Salford University
  • North Wales Management School

After weeks of research related to taking up a Master's course, course durations and costs, I concluded the following average statistics that helped guide my decision:

~£15 000

Cost

~2 years

Duration

Given a few data points to consider:

  1. high average costs,
  2. duration of taking a postgraduate course full-time,
  3. familiarity with the subject matter,
  4. previous degree requirements,
  5. work experience

My search then narrowed down to Essex University (Online). The university provides a MSc Computer Science course available in smaller chunks and also considered my work experience that now permits me to take up study based. I feel that universities in UK requiring students to have recent degrees (with qualification levels such as "2:1" or "2:2" is disadvantageous to potential students, because people who want to study, may have studied many years ago and those age-old qualifications are deemed no longer valid)
